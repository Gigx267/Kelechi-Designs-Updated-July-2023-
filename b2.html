Creative Illustrations In Frontend

Using creative illustrations in frontend development can significantly enhance the visual appeal and user experience of your website or application. Illustrations can make your frontend more engaging, expressive and memorable. Here are some sites on how to incorporate creative illustrations effectively:

UnDraw\ Be it branding, podcasting, online transactions, app development, data analysis, creator and founder Katerina Limpitsouni has got you covered. The website is straightforward and easy to use.

Open Doodles/ by Pablo Stanley offers free sketchy illustrations ideal for those looking to add hand-drawn elements on their web pages. Free for commercial use under CC0 license, these doodles are available in SVG, PNG and animated GIF formats.

Ouch by Icons8 /provides catchy illustrations in 20 different art styles – from flat and geometric illustrations to textured graphics and cartoonish vectors. Illustrations are sorted by different categories including Technologies, E-commerce, Finance, and Medicine as well as popular UX states such as Success, Message Sent, Sign up, Subscribed etc.

Flaticon /is the world’s biggest stockpile of icons, with over 2 million vector icons. The illustrations are all free for anyone to use. Flaticon’s vectors are organized into “packs” of various themes and topics for users’ ease. Lastly, all icons created for Google Docs and Slides are customizable!

Openclipart /is a treasure trove of up to 160,000 clipart available for free use. The illustrations available come in a variety of styles and aesthetics, so there is something for everyone!

Glaze offers a library of free illustrations under different categories such as people, backgrounds, places and technology. Download includes free PNG files that you can use with credit with an option to purchase the source AI file.
